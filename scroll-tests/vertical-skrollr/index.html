The

Empire State Building

Scroll Down

The Empire State Building was completed in 1931. Its name is from "Empire State", the nickname of the state of New York.

The building has a roof height of 1,250 feet (380 m) and stands a total of 1,454 feet (443.2 m) tall, including its antenna. It was the world's tallest building until 1970, and today is the world's 54th tallest building.

Floors 1 through 85 contain over 2 million square feet (200,500 m2) of commercial and office space. The 86th floor contains an observatory. Another 16 floors are part of the spire, with a second observatory on the 102nd floor.

As a world-famous symbol of New York City, the building has been named as one of the Seven Wonders of the Modern World by the American Society of Civil Engineers.
